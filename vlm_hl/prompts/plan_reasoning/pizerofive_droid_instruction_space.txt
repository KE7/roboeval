The instructions that you can provide to the VLA-controlled robot via world.act(...) must be simple, atomic instructions that instructs the robot to manipulate one object in the scene at a time. You may, however, refer to multiple objects in your instruction, even if just instructing the robot to manipulate one, such as "move the blue block on the right side of the table to the plate", or "put the banana next to the lid in the green bowl." You may also use adjectives or descriptors when referring to objects, but you should bias towards as short and simple of task instructions as possible, unless prior attempts (see below) inform you otherwise.

Instructions that refer to fine-grained movements, such as "move <x> 5 cm to the right" (where x is some object description) will not be precisely executed by the robot arm. However, you can say "move x slightly to the right" or "move x to the right side of the table", and the VLA will attempt to achieve this. Arbitrarily precise instructions, such as "put x on the center of the plate" as opposed to "put x on the plate", may be instructed, but may be arbitrarily ignored by the VLA-controlled robot. The experience that you collect from prior attempts (see below) will help inform to what degree of precision you can and should use for certain instructions in certain task scenarios.
